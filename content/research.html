Do Election Campaigns Affect Leaders' Behavior?

JMP

Muhammad Arslan Iqbal, Nisvan Erkal, Aaron Kamm

Abstract: This paper examines how campaign promises influence elected leaders' behavior using a laboratory experiment where leaders set voters' tax rates and then decide their own contribution to a common fund. Leaders can either contribute to or withdraw (embezzle) from the fund. With non-binding campaigns, candidates make generous promises but renege and engage in embezzlement once elected. Making campaigns binding does not improve voter welfare or leader responsibility.

Do Leader Selection Mechanisms Affect Leaders' Behavior?

Working Paper

Muhammad Arslan Iqbal, Nisvan Erkal, Aaron Kamm

Abstract: This study compares how random appointment, meritocracy, and election influence leaders' behavior in environments with conflicting interests. Most leaders act opportunistically regardless of selection method. Meritocracy and election increase tax evasion relative to random appointment.
