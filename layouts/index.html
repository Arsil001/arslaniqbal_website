About

Biography

I'm a PhD Economics candidate at the University of Melbourne, specializing in experimental and behavioral economics. My research focuses on understanding how election campaigns, selection mechanisms, and institutional frameworks influence leader behavior and decision-making.

Through innovative laboratory experiments, I investigate mechanisms that encourage responsible leadership and welfare-optimizing reforms, particularly in contexts with weak institutional frameworks for accountability. My work combines rigorous experimental design with insights from behavioral science to inform policy and organizational practices.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned a Gold Medal for my MSc in Economics from the University of the Punjab and worked as an Assistant Director at the State Bank of Pakistan. I've also held teaching positions at several institutions, fostering the next generation of economic thinkers.
